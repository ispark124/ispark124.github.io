running & biking

July 14, 2020

It's been a little bit over a week and I'm really starting to run out of ideas here. I think it's good to make this a habit for me, writing on here every day, but it feels like a really long run that won't really end.

Recently I've been trying to live out a better story and I think today I kinda did just that. Not that I did anything crazy or anything, but I went out and did something spontaneous, which is something I haven't done for a while. After my practice in the morning for me and a few other kids that aren't cleared to run with the school yet, I thought I'd bike over to the middle school and catch the end of practice with the rest of my team and talk with my coach. When I got there, my team wasn't there but on the way there I ran into a few of my friends who were also heading to the middle school. I've known these guys for a while but it's never really been anything super close or anything, just people to be around in school. Soon we got to the middle school and a few other kids were pulling up, apparently they were all gonna run and work out together. My team or coach wasn't on the field, but the girls soccer team was on and both me and the other group of guys ended up coming for no reason. They couldn't run with the girls on the field and I missed my team leaving. We ended up talking some more and they were deciding where to go to run. I had no plan of going with these guys but an opportunity presented itself and I wanted another story to add to my book and wanting more relationship so I went with them.

We decided to bike to River Dell high school because I knew that their track was open because I went yesterday so we biked. Now the bike ride was something I knew I was gonna write about later. These kids rode crazy. Recently I've been doing my fair share of bike riding, probably more than I've been running, but that's only been to ride around without a particular direction and just enjoying the ride. These guys rode as if they were trying to die. Obviously they know exactly when to cross streets and what side of the road to be on, they're way too smart to actually get hit, but the way they rode is nothing like what I'd ride. I really don't like going on long bike rides with my family because I have to slow down so often, but with these guys I felt amazing. We'd weave through cars (not on sidewalks?!), cross through streets (not on crosswalks?!), and speed through it all. I felt alive.

Well actually once we got to the high school, some guy in a golf cart (probably their trainor because ours rides around in one too), told us that we had to get off campus because their teams were practicing there. That was great, we biked there for no reason. So we decided to go to this other field that my friend originally wanted to go to. Once again riding through the streets until we got where we wanted to be. I knew they were there to run and play soccer which worked out perfectly because I only ran a few miles right before at practice, and I was planning on running again later at night to get a higher mileage for the day. Instead I thought I'd run few more miles with them.

I ended up spending a few more hours with them playing soccer, and eventually biking back to the middle school, and doing it all before 12:00. Looking back, I guess it doesn't look like all that much. If it was anyone else, it'd look like a normal day of hanging out with some friends. But for me I think it was more. It was me stepping out to do something intentional. To put something on the page of my book. I may not be booking a flight the day of and flying out just for the fun of it, but this is intentional steps towards doing more. I may not be super close with these guys but more relationship can begin to form and actually I think one of the guys is gonna invite me next time they go to run. I may not remember this day at all in a few years down the line, maybe I will, but what will matter then is how I'll be living my life. Will it be like I did today, stepping out without any plans and biking with zeal, or will I decide to go home because I didn't bring food and I feel tired? Hopefully I'll be living a real life and I think everyone knows which of the two that is.

Earlier I said that writing every day is like a really long run that won't end. I'm realizing now (not for the first time), that all I have to do is be intentional with my day and be able to listen in every circumstance. If I can even learn from these guys, who I can say are pretty far from choosing to go to church over a houseparty, that I can live a little more freely even in the way that I bike, how hard can listening for God be? Also really long runs? I'm a distance runner, I was born for this ish.
